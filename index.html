The weekend

What it actually feels like.

No experience needed. Just curiosity, a pair of legs that walk, and a willingness to be a little uncomfortable in the best way.

Friday afternoon

You arrive with your pack.

We meet, drop the awkward small talk, and walk into the trees together. Everything you need for the next two days is on your back — and after the first hour you'll be surprised how little that actually is.

[ Image — trailhead, women shouldering packs ]

Saturday evening

Dinner cooks on a tiny stove.

Tents are up, the kettle's on, and someone has just filtered water from a stream — which still feels a bit like magic. We share food and stories around the stove, then a card game until the light goes. The night sky does the rest.

[ Image — camp dinner, golden light ]

Sunday morning

Coffee circle in the grass.

Slow start. Hot coffee, no phones, a small gratitude moment before we pack down. By the time we walk out, something has shifted — you've slept outside, carried your own life on your shoulders, and made the kind of friends that text you about the next trip before this one is over.

[ Image — morning coffee circle ]
Who it's for

If you've ever thought "I'd love to do that" — this is for you.

Maybe you save every wild-camping post you see, but you've never actually slept outside. Maybe you'd go tomorrow if a friend asked, but no one is asking. Maybe the gear list looks intimidating, or maybe you just don't want to figure it all out by yourself.

You don't need to be super fit. You don't need to own a single piece of kit. You don't need to have done this before. You only need to be curious — and to want, even a little, to surprise yourself.

That's it. That's the whole requirement.

What you get

Everything sorted, so you can just show up.

You don't have to plan the route, pick the spot, or worry about whether you're packing the right things. I've done all of that — so the only thing left for you is to say yes and turn up.

Before the weekend

A personal packing list, advice on what to borrow or skip, and an optional group call so you arrive knowing me and the other women.

During the weekend

A scouted route, a wild spot to camp, a small mini-workshop on solo camping, and me — there the whole time so you're never figuring it out alone.

The little things

A WhatsApp group to land in, help organising a carpool, an opening circle, card games at camp, and a slow morning coffee before we walk out.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Do I need experience?

No. This weekend is designed for beginners. If you've never wild camped before, you're exactly who this is for.

What gear do I need?

You'll get a full packing list once you sign up. Most items are things you might already have or can borrow. We'll talk through the essentials together before the trip.

What if I don't have equipment?

No problem. We help with materials advice, can point you to rental options, and in some cases gear can be borrowed or sponsored. Don't let kit be the reason you don't come.

Is it safe?

Yes. A host is with you the whole weekend. Routes and camping spots are scouted in advance. Small groups mean nobody gets lost in the shuffle.

What if it rains?

We go anyway — that's part of it. You'll be prepped with the right gear, and honestly, a rainy night in a tent makes for the best stories. If the weather is genuinely unsafe, we adapt.

How fit do I need to be?

If you can walk for a few hours with a pack, you're fine. Routes are chosen to be doable, not punishing.
