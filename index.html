The weekend

How it actually goes.

No experience needed. Just curiosity, a pair of legs that walk, and a willingness to be a little uncomfortable in the best way.

Saturday morning

We meet at the start of the route.

After a short introduction, one last check of the backpacks and a run-through of the plan, we head into nature together. For some it's the first time walking with everything on your back. Exciting, maybe a little nerve-wracking — which is exactly why we do it together.

We take our time, get to know each other, hike for a few hours, and stop for lunch somewhere with a view.

images/saturday-morning.jpg

Saturday evening

We arrive at the wild camp.

Tents go up, water gets filtered from the stream, and we wash off the day in the river. Then dinner cooks on a tiny stove, and we eat together as the light goes soft.

No screens. No rush. No full agenda. Just the quiet of being outside, good conversation, and the strange, beautiful experience of falling asleep under the stars.

images/saturday-evening.jpg

Sunday

We wake up in the middle of nature.

Maybe with a coffee in the morning sun, maybe with a bit of mist between the trees. After breakfast we pack up camp and keep walking — more time for conversation, a few more skills along the way, and a lot of looking around.

By late afternoon we're back at the end of the trail. Tired legs, new memories, and already wondering when the next one is.

  • You want to spend more time in nature — properly, not just a Sunday walk.
  • You love trying small adventures: hiking, wild camping, sleeping out.

If even one of those felt familiar — you're in the right place.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Is this only for beginners?

Not at all. Whether it's your first night outside or your fiftieth, you'll fit in. Some women come for the experience, some for the community, some just want a weekend in nature with other people who get it.

What gear do I need?

Most things are items you might already have — a backpack, a sleeping bag, walking shoes. For anything you're missing, I can help arrange rentals or point you to gear worth borrowing.

What about food?

Dinner on Saturday and breakfast on Sunday are included. You bring your own lunch and snacks for the trail — I'll send guidance on what works well when you're walking with a pack.

Is it safe?

Yes. I'm with you the whole weekend, routes and camping spots are scouted in advance, and small groups mean nobody gets lost in the shuffle.

What if it rains?

We go anyway — that's part of it. You'll be prepped with the right gear, and honestly, a rainy night in a tent makes for the best stories. If the weather is genuinely unsafe, we adapt.

How fit do I need to be?

If you can walk for a few hours with a pack, you're set. Routes are chosen to be enjoyable, not punishing — we move at a pace where everyone can chat and look around.
